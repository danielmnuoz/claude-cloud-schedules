Iran / Middle East Conflict — Day 35+

  • US-Israel military strikes on Iran began February 28; the conflict is now entering its sixth week with no clear off-ramp. The Strait of Hormuz closure has stranded ~20 mb/d of Gulf oil exports — IEA calls it the "largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market."
  • Brent crude ~$120/barrel. Saudi Arabia warns prices could reach $180/barrel if disruptions continue through late April, implying US gasoline above $6/gallon. A US waiver allowing sale of Russian oil loaded before March 12 expires April 11.
  • Trump threatened to hit Iran "extremely hard" — each escalatory statement generates fresh market volatility. A fragile Gaza ceasefire is largely holding.

Ukraine / Russia

  • The EU extended Ukraine-related sanctions through September 15, 2026. War continues with no near-term resolution; Kyrgyzstan is threatening legal action over EU sanctions compliance ambiguity on re-exports to Russia.

Trade Policy & Tariffs

  • The US Supreme Court struck down Trump's IEEPA tariffs (reciprocal + fentanyl-related), ruling he lacked authority — but Section 301 China tariffs and sector tariffs remain. Average effective US tariff rate fell from ~27% to ~13.7%.
  • Consumer impacts are peaking April–October 2026 (12–18-month lag from Liberation Day). Food prices already up 1.6% — equal to an entire prior year of grocery inflation. Trump has signaled intent to raise some tariffs back toward 15%.
