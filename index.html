Iran War — Day 35 (Critical Week Ahead)

  • The US–Israel military campaign against Iran entered its 35th day. Trump said he expects "two to three more weeks" of major operations but also described the war as "nearly done" in an April 1 primetime address.
  • Hormuz Deadline: Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum Friday warning "all hell will rain down" on Iran if the Strait of Hormuz is not reopened. The deadline expires Monday, April 6 — the same day US markets reopen — making this the highest near-term risk catalyst for oil and equities.
  • Iran claimed it shot down two US warplanes on Friday (Apr 4). The US has not confirmed losses; a search for a missing US airman is underway.
  • Gulf nations backed a UN Security Council resolution authorizing "all necessary measures" to reopen Hormuz (Apr 2). Indirect US–Iran negotiations via Pakistan, Egypt, and Turkey have produced no breakthrough.
  • Separately, Israel bombed bridges in Lebanon as the 2026 Lebanon conflict continues; over 1,000 militants and civilians killed since late March.

Trade, Sanctions & Diplomacy

  • Special envoy Steve Witkoff floated a 15-point Iran peace proposal; Tehran has not formally responded and denied pursuing a ceasefire.
  • Maersk and other shipping firms are rerouting around the Cape of Good Hope as Hormuz traffic has largely halted, adding weeks to Asia–Europe transit times and driving freight rates sharply higher.
  • Roughly one-third of Asia–Europe air freight capacity — normally routed over the Middle East — remains disrupted, creating widespread cargo delays.

Tech & Business

Layoffs & Workforce Restructuring

  • Oracle announced the largest single layoff event of 2026 — an estimated 20,000–30,000 jobs (~25% of workforce) — communicated via a brief 6 AM email. TD Cowen estimates up to $10B in savings; freed capital is earmarked for AI data center investment. Oracle stock is down ~25% since January.
  • Block (Jack Dorsey) eliminated 4,000 jobs (~40% of staff) in early March, explicitly attributing the cuts to expanding AI capabilities — described as the single largest AI-attributed layoff event in tech history.
  • Total Q1 2026 tech layoffs exceeded 50,000 jobs; AI was cited as a driver in at least 20–25% of cuts. Meta, Google, Amazon, Atlassian, Pinterest, and Salesforce all made significant reductions while referencing AI productivity gains.

AI & Industry Trends

  • AI is now the top reason employers cite for tech layoffs, accounting for ~15,300 of March job cuts alone. Analysts debate how much is genuine automation vs. "AI-washing" to justify cost reduction and fund infrastructure spending.
  • The sector-wide pattern: trade headcount for compute. Oracle, Block, and others are reinvesting workforce savings into AI infrastructure even as revenue growth slows.
