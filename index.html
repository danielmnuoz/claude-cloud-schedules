International Markets & Geopolitics

Global Markets

  • Europe: Pan-European Stoxx 600 fell 0.7% Tuesday afternoon as the Iran ceasefire expiry loomed. Energy-heavy indices remain most exposed to the oil supply shock from the Hormuz closure.
  • Asia: China's CSI 300 gained 0.61% and Japan's Nikkei 225 rose 0.6% Tuesday, buoyed briefly by war-resolution optimism. A ceasefire resolution and Hormuz reopening would likely trigger a strong Asian equity rally.

Iran–US Conflict BREAKING / DAY 54

  • Ceasefire extended, but fragile: President Trump extended the two-week ceasefire — originally set to expire Wednesday — at Pakistan's request, saying it will remain in place until Iran submits a proposal and "discussions are concluded, one way or the other." Iran refuses to negotiate while the US blockade of its ports remains in place.
  • Strait of Hormuz crisis continues: Only ~12 vessels crossed the Strait in the past 24 hours. Iran's IRGC seized two vessels attempting to exit on Wednesday morning and fired on a third container ship without warning, per the UK Maritime Trade Operations Centre. The blockade is driving oil to $95/barrel.
  • Trajectory: The conflict remains at a dangerous impasse. A breakthrough could sharply deflate oil prices and spark an equity rally; an escalation risks broader regional war. Markets are pricing in a prolonged standoff.

Trade, Sanctions & Diplomacy

  • US–China tariffs: China faces an effective tariff rate of 31.6% — the highest of any major US trading partner. USTR Section 301 public hearings on China trade practices are scheduled for April 28. The Iran conflict is complicating any broader US–China trade deal momentum.
  • Pharma tariffs: A Trump April 2 proclamation set a 100% duty on patented pharmaceuticals (reduced to 20% for companies committing to onshore production), adding cost pressure to healthcare supply chains.
  • IEEPA tariff refunds: US Customs launched an automated refund-request system on April 20 to streamline IEEPA tariff rebates, offering some relief to importers.

Tech & Business

  • Apple CEO transition: Apple officially announced that Tim Cook will become Executive Chairman and John Ternus (SVP of Hardware Engineering, a 20-year Apple veteran) will become CEO effective September 1, 2026. Cook has overseen Apple's market cap grow from ~$350B to ~$4T since 2011. Markets and industry figures — including Trump, Altman, and Buffett — have commented on the move.
  • Tech layoffs accelerate — AI restructuring: The tech sector has now shed more than 150,000 jobs across 500+ companies in 2026; Q1 alone saw ~80,000 cuts with nearly half explicitly attributed to AI automation. Oracle leads with ~25,000 positions eliminated (~6% of workforce), with freed capital earmarked for AI data center expansion.
  • Snap and Epic cuts: Snap is cutting 1,000 employees, citing AI reducing "repetitive work." Epic Games is eliminating 1,000 roles (~20% of its workforce), blaming a sustained decline in Fortnite engagement since 2025.
  • Big Tech AI investment: Amazon, Meta, Google, and Microsoft are collectively expected to invest $650 billion in AI infrastructure in 2026 — data centers, chips, networking, and energy. The pattern is clear: layoffs in legacy divisions fund AI expansion.
  • Earnings watch — AI bellwethers: ServiceNow (enterprise AI adoption signal) and IBM report this week; the mega-cap AI quartet — Alphabet, Meta, Apple, and Amazon — all report April 29–30, making next week the most consequential stretch of the earnings season for AI sentiment.
