Iran War — Day 35 (Developing)

  • Joint US-Israeli strikes on Iran began February 28, targeting nuclear infrastructure, ballistic missile sites, and naval assets. Israeli-US forces killed Iranian Supreme Leader Khamenei and Secretary of the Supreme National Security Council Larijani.
  • Iran retaliated by closing the Strait of Hormuz and striking US bases and allied assets across the region. Hezbollah launched cross-border attacks (Israeli response killed 1,250+ and displaced 1M+); Houthis joined the war March 28 with a ballistic missile strike toward Israel.
  • Trump addressed the nation April 1, saying the war is "nearing completion" and projecting another 2–3 weeks of US involvement — markets recovered briefly then fell again as no firm deadline was given.
  • Hormuz closure is likely permanent for 2026; shipping analysts say routine transit will not resume this year. Jet fuel has more than doubled, forcing airlines including Air New Zealand and Vietnam Airlines to cut flights.

Ukraine War — Day ~1,500

  • Russia declared it "completed the liberation" of Luhansk oblast (Ukraine had controlled only 0.2%). A major Russian spring offensive is signaled after a brief lull; ISW assesses Moscow is preparing its 2026 campaign.
  • Ukraine drone strikes have knocked out ~40% of Russia's western oil export capacity. Russia responded with a 700-drone barrage (two waves) on the night of March 31–April 1. Total Russian combat losses exceed 1.3 million personnel.
