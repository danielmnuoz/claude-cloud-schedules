Iran War (Day 59)

  • Strait of Hormuz remains closed; new Iranian proposal on the table. Iran has submitted a new proposal to the US aimed at reopening the strait and ending the war, but terms are disputed. The closure continues to choke ~20% of global oil supply, sending WTI above $95 and Brent above $107.
  • Diplomacy in flux. Trump canceled the US envoy trip to Pakistan (Apr 25) after Iran refused demands on uranium enrichment. Iran's FM Araghchi flew to Moscow (Day 58) to meet Putin, who has publicly expressed support for Tehran — complicating the US-led negotiating track.
  • Military posture escalating. Three US aircraft carriers are now stationed in the Middle East simultaneously — the first time since 2003 — underscoring the military pressure campaign. Israeli-Hezbollah exchanges in Lebanon remain active (at least 2,509 killed since March 2).
  • Trajectory: Back-channel talks continue and both sides are described as "not as far apart as they seem," but no deal appears imminent. Markets are pricing in an extended disruption scenario, pushing oil higher and weighing on European industrials.

Trade & Diplomatic Developments

  • US-China at a delicate inflection point. The Busan trade truce (signed Oct 2025) expires November 10; Trump's March visit to Beijing yielded limited deliverables, and Beijing has responded with its own MOFCOM probes and a sweeping April 7 supply chain security regulation targeting foreign companies.
  • European leaders courting Beijing. Leaders of Ireland, Canada, Finland, and the UK have all visited Beijing in 2026, seeking stronger economic ties — a sign of diverging transatlantic trade strategies as European nations hedge against US tariff volatility.

Tech & Business

  • AI-driven tech layoffs accelerating sharply. The tech industry cut nearly 80,000 workers in Q1 2026 alone — roughly 48% of those cuts explicitly attributed to AI automation replacing roles in content, customer support, data entry, and basic coding. Total tech job losses since 2020 are approaching 900,000.
  • Meta: 8,000 layoffs (~10% of workforce) announced Thursday April 23, with cuts beginning May 20; Meta is simultaneously scrapping 6,000 open roles. The move is framed as an "AI efficiency push" ahead of what is expected to be a strong Q1 earnings print Wednesday.
  • Oracle: 30,000 layoffs — the single largest tech workforce reduction of 2026 — as the company consolidates around cloud AI infrastructure.
  • Snap: ~1,000 layoffs (16% of workforce) announced this month as the social platform trims costs amid ad market competition from AI-native products.
  • Nike: ~1,400 layoffs, concentrated in its technology division, as the company pulls back on internal digital build-out.
  • AI capex paradox intact. Alphabet, Microsoft, Meta, and Amazon are collectively projected to spend ~$700 billion on AI infrastructure in 2026 — a record — even as they cut headcount at scale. AI engineering roles remain in strong demand; generalist IT and middle-management roles are contracting.
  • Big Tech earnings on deck Wednesday. GOOGL, AMZN, META, and MSFT all report Q1 on April 29 — the most concentrated "Magnificent Four" day of the year. AI capex guidance and cloud growth rates will be the primary market-moving disclosures.
