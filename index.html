International Markets & Geopolitics

2026 Iran War & Strait of Hormuz Crisis — Day 33

Critical deadline: April 6 — Trump has threatened extensive strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure if the Strait of Hormuz is not reopened by Monday.
  • Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz closed on March 4, triggering what the IEA describes as the largest oil supply disruption in the history of the global energy market. The world is losing ~4.5–5M barrels/day (~5% of global supply), a figure analysts warn could double by mid-April.
  • Latest battlefield (April 1): Iran's Revolutionary Guards fired three waves of missiles at Israel — 16 wounded in Tel Aviv/Bnei Brak. US continued airstrikes on underground military targets in Tehran and Isfahan. 10 US service members injured at Prince Sultan Air Base, Saudi Arabia (no fatalities). Houthi militants have joined the fighting.
  • Diplomatic signals — mixed: Trump says Iran agreed to "most of" the US's 15-point demand list and the war could end in "2–3 weeks." Iran's foreign minister publicly rejected the plan and called Trump "deceitful," stating Iran is prepared for "at least 6 months" of war.
  • US response measures: 400M barrel strategic reserve release (record), temporary sanctions lifting on some Russian/Iranian oil, naval escort mission (Operation Aspides). Qatar's LNG plant sustained missile damage — repairs may take up to 5 years.

Ukraine–Russia

  • Russia continues to advance around Pokrovsk and Siversk (Donetsk), Huliaipole (Zaporizhzhia), and Kupiansk (Kharkiv). Ukraine lost its Kursk foothold in spring and is suffering attrition, with its power grid heavily damaged.
  • Key risk: Trump reportedly willing to push a "skewed deal" favoring Russia. EU/UK/US enacted new sanctions on Russia's largest oil producers, though enforcement is complicated by Iran war energy pressures.

Trade & Diplomacy

  • Trump–Xi Beijing summit delayed by ~1 month from its original March 31–April 2 dates due to the Iran war. Expected deliverables remain: extend the US-China trade truce (currently through November 2026) by 1 year and Chinese soybean purchases. A $13B Taiwan weapons sale was reportedly withheld to avoid derailing talks.
  • Tariffs: Average effective US tariff rate is ~13.7% (February 2026), down from ~27% peak after the February 20 Supreme Court 6-3 ruling that IEEPA does not authorize presidential tariffs. CBP ordered to process importer refunds. USMCA first joint review scheduled for July 2026.
