US–Iran War — Day 57 (Developing)

  • Background: On February 28, 2026, the US and Israel launched airstrikes on Iran targeting military sites and senior officials including Supreme Leader Khamenei; Iran responded with missile strikes across the region and closed the Strait of Hormuz, blocking ~20% of global crude and LNG flows.
  • President Trump extended the ceasefire by three weeks on April 21; US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner flew to Islamabad this weekend for direct peace talks with Iran mediated by Pakistan — the highest-level US–Iran contact since the war began.
  • Iran's foreign minister has agreed to meet Pakistani mediators; markets are cautiously optimistic, though analysts warn a resumption of hostilities remains the base case if the blockade precondition is not resolved.
  • Trajectory: A successful Islamabad meeting could be a significant catalyst for an oil price pullback and equity rally; failure would likely push Brent above $110 and reprice global risk assets.

Trade, Sanctions & Diplomacy

  • The UK and Turkey signed a new Strategic Partnership Framework on April 23, strengthening bilateral security and economic cooperation with emphasis on Middle East stability — a notable NATO realignment move.
  • US-China tariffs remain at 20% on most goods under an extension through November 2026; following the Supreme Court's February ruling barring IEEPA tariff authority, the administration is operating under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974.
  • Canada opened anti-dumping and countervailing duty investigations into Chinese plywood on April 10, part of a broader Western effort to counter Chinese trade diversion.
