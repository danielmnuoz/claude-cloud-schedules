US–Iran War (Day 45 — Developing)

  • Blockade begins: After weekend peace talks in Islamabad failed to reach agreement, Trump ordered the US Navy to blockade Iranian ports and the Strait of Hormuz; the blockade began 10 AM ET on April 13. Iran's IRGC has threatened retaliation against all ports in the Persian Gulf and Gulf of Oman.
  • Diplomacy signal: Tuesday markets partially recovered after Trump indicated openness to further negotiations before the current truce fully collapses — the critical question is whether a longer-term ceasefire deal can be struck. The UN warns the conflict has already pushed ~30 million people toward poverty worldwide.
  • Lebanon / Hezbollah: Israel struck Lebanon on April 8 killing at least 357 people, maintaining these operations fall outside the US-Iran ceasefire framework — a position disputed by mediators. Roughly 600,000 remain displaced in southern Lebanon; Iraq has also become an increasingly active front with 80+ pro-Iran militia strike events in March alone.

Trade Policy

  • IEEPA tariffs struck down: The Supreme Court invalidated Trump's broad IEEPA-based tariffs; the government is working to refund an estimated $166B collected from more than 330,000 businesses. A coalition of 24 attorneys general has sued to block the replacement tariffs as well.
  • New 10% global tariff: Trump invoked Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 to impose a 10% global tariff, in effect through July 24, 2026. USMCA-covered imports (Canada/Mexico) remain exempt indefinitely.
  • China threat: Trump threatened 50% tariffs on China after reports surfaced Sunday that Beijing was preparing a weapons shipment to Iran; analysts view implementation as legally complex post-IEEPA ruling and consider it largely an empty threat for now.
