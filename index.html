Fed & Monetary Policy

  • The FOMC voted to hold the benchmark rate at 3.50%–3.75% on April 29 — a third consecutive pause — citing elevated inflation tied to Iran-conflict energy prices and tariff uncertainty.
  • This was Jerome Powell's final FOMC meeting as Chair; he steps down in mid-May. Kevin Warsh is set to take over. Internal dissent: Governor Miran voted for a 25 bp cut; Governors Hammack, Kashkari, and Logan supported the hold but opposed any easing bias in the statement.
  • Markets have dialed back rate-cut expectations for 2026 and are beginning to price in a possible rate hike in 2027 if the Hormuz-driven oil shock proves persistent.

Iran War — Day 61 (Developing)

  • The US-Israel war with Iran entered its 61st day. The US military blockade of Iranian ports and near-closure of the Strait of Hormuz — which normally carries ~20% of global energy flows — has triggered what the IEA called the largest supply shock on record. Thousands of seafarers remain stranded in the Gulf.
  • Peace talks remain stalled. Iran's FM Araghchi met Putin in Moscow; Trump told Tehran to "just give up" and said negotiations are proceeding "telephonically." Iran's latest proposal would reopen Hormuz but defer nuclear discussions — Secretary Rubio called it "better than expected" but not yet sufficient.
  • The Lebanon ceasefire is fraying: Israel and Hezbollah have escalated exchanges despite a US-brokered extension, with at least 2,509 killed in Lebanon since March 2.
  • Trajectory: A diplomatic breakthrough is the biggest single upside catalyst for energy markets; further escalation and a collapsed ceasefire is the primary tail risk for Q2, with Brent potentially surging well past $120/bbl.

Tech & Business

  • AI-driven layoffs accelerating: ~100,000 tech workers have been cut in 2026 so far, with nearly 48% of 2026 layoffs explicitly attributed to AI automation — the first year AI displacement has overtaken other restructuring rationales as the primary cause. Oracle led with ~30,000 cuts; Snap axed 16% of its workforce (~1,000 roles), openly stating 65% of its code is now AI-generated.
  • Meta announced 8,000 layoffs (10% headcount, effective May 20) while simultaneously guiding $115–135B in 2026 AI capex — a 67–97% YoY increase. Q1 revenue surged 33% to $56.3B, demonstrating AI is simultaneously eliminating jobs and supercharging revenue.
  • Microsoft's AI business hit a $37B annual run rate (+123% YoY), with Azure accelerating to +40% growth — the strongest Azure quarter in years, signaling enterprise AI adoption is inflecting sharply upward. Microsoft also offered buyouts to ~7% of its US workforce.
  • Alphabet beat on Cloud ($20B, +28%) and advertising ($77.3B, +15.5%), with net income surging 81% — confirming that AI search enhancements and cloud are compounding simultaneously rather than cannibalizing one another.
  • A new study warns of an "automation trap": companies cutting workers to fund AI may face productivity shortfalls if AI systems underperform expectations, creating structural competitiveness risk.
