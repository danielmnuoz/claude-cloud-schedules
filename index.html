Iran War — Day 56 (Developing)

  • President Trump extended the US-Iran ceasefire by three weeks on April 21, offering time for Tehran to submit a peace proposal; the Israel-Lebanon truce was also extended three weeks following White House meetings on April 24.
  • Iran rejected the extension as meaningless, stating that the ongoing US naval blockade of Iranian ports is itself a ceasefire violation — negotiators will not return to the table until the blockade is lifted.
  • The Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed, disrupting roughly 20% of global crude and LNG shipments for nearly two months; this is the primary driver of elevated energy prices.
  • Trajectory: Talks are stalled on the blockade precondition. A ceasefire collapse before the extended deadline is the key tail risk for markets heading into next week.

Trade & Tariffs

  • US-China: A 20% tariff on most Chinese imports remains in effect (bilateral deal extended through Nov 2026); after the Supreme Court's Feb 2026 ruling barred using IEEPA for tariffs, the administration shifted to Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974.
  • Trump's China strategy is widely described as "adrift" — officials have shifted toward "managed trade" and stability as tariffs have not meaningfully altered Chinese trade or military behavior.
  • The EU unveiled emergency measures to cushion its energy markets from the West Asia crisis impact.
