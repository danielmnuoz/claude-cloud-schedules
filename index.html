International Markets & Geopolitics

US-Iran Ceasefire & Strait of Hormuz (Day 42 — Developing)

  • A two-week US-Iran ceasefire brokered by Pakistan took effect April 8, hours before Trump's threatened deadline; the deal requires Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, through which ~12–15 million barrels/day had been blocked — the largest oil supply shock on record.
  • Implementation remains deeply fragile: oil tanker traffic through the strait was halted again within hours of the first passage, as Iran cited ongoing Israeli strikes on Lebanon (not covered by ceasefire terms). Brent crude sits at ~$100–101/barrel — well below the peak but far above the pre-war ~$70 level.
  • Formal US-Iran peace negotiations opened today in Islamabad under Pakistani mediation; Trump described Iran's 10-point peace proposal as "a workable basis." A breakdown would send oil sharply higher and reverse the April 8 equity rally.

Israel-Lebanon War

  • On April 8, Israel launched Operation Eternal Darkness — a massive air and artillery campaign — killing 254+ people in Lebanon in a single day; the IDF called it its most powerful attacks on Lebanon yet, targeting Hezbollah sites.
  • Netanyahu and the US both stated Lebanon is not included in the Iran ceasefire, contradicting Pakistan's mediating position; Hezbollah launched retaliatory missiles into northern Israel on April 9 before declaring it had halted new attacks. Direct Israel-Lebanon talks are scheduled for this weekend (Apr 11–12).
  • The war has killed more than 1,500 people in Lebanon and displaced nearly 1 million (20% of the population) since hostilities began March 2.
