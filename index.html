US–Iran War (Day 51) — Critical Week

  • Background: On February 28, the U.S. and Israel launched Operation Epic Fury — coordinated airstrikes targeting Iran's military sites, nuclear facilities, and leadership; a two-week ceasefire was agreed April 7–8 and expires Wednesday, April 22.
  • Latest escalation (Apr 19): A U.S. Navy guided-missile destroyer fired on and seized an Iranian-flagged cargo vessel in the Gulf of Oman; Iran is calling it a ceasefire violation and has vowed retaliation. Tehran says the Strait of Hormuz remains closed until the U.S. lifts its port blockade.
  • Strait of Hormuz closed since Apr 18: Iran shut the strait after the U.S. refused to end its blockade; two Indian-flagged VLCCs were fired upon by Iranian gunboats. Global oil inventories fell 85 million barrels in March; demand is projected to contract 2.3 mb/d in April.
  • Trajectory: The ceasefire expiry Wednesday is the single biggest near-term binary risk for markets. A breakdown would push oil to $100+ again, hammer equities, and spike gold and Treasuries. Extension remains possible but fragile.

Trade Policy & Tariffs

  • Pharmaceutical tariffs (Apr 2 proclamation): 100% ad valorem duty on patented pharmaceuticals and active ingredients, effective July–September 2026. Companies committing to domestic production get a 20% rate, rising to 100% by April 2030 if onshoring doesn't occur.
  • Metal tariffs: Additional Section 232 duties of 10–50% on aluminum, copper, and steel derivatives took effect April 6.
  • Section 301 hearings: USTR opened 60 investigations into forced-labor import enforcement; public hearings on China/EU structural overcapacity are scheduled for April 28.
  • US–Ecuador trade deal was signed March 13 — a bright spot in an otherwise tense trade landscape.
