US–Iran War — Day 47 (Critical Developing Situation)

  • Ceasefire expiration: April 21. The two-week truce agreed April 7–8 (brokered by Pakistan) expires in six days. Weekend peace talks in Islamabad collapsed after 20+ hours; sticking points include Iran’s nuclear program, Hormuz shipping terms, and unfreezing Iranian assets.
  • Hormuz blockade active. The US Navy is blocking all traffic to/from Iranian ports. Trump warned any vessels approaching the blockade "will be immediately eliminated." The blockade has disrupted global shipping lanes and caused fuel shortages in parts of Asia.
  • New talks possible. Trump said Tuesday the war is "close to over" and that talks could resume within two days; the White House confirmed a second negotiating round is under active discussion but nothing is officially scheduled. Markets are pricing in an optimistic scenario.
  • Lebanon / Hezbollah front active. Lebanon was not included in the ceasefire. Israeli forces and Hezbollah continue to exchange fire; Lebanon’s health authorities report 2,000+ killed since March 2, with 600,000+ displaced in the south.

Trade, Tariffs & Diplomacy

  • China tariff escalation threat. Trump threatened 50% tariffs on China following reports of a Chinese arms shipment to Iran. Near-term implementation is legally uncertain post the Supreme Court’s IEEPA ruling; analysts treat it as a negotiating signal for now.
  • 10% global tariff in effect through July 24. After IEEPA-based tariffs were struck down by the Supreme Court, Trump imposed a 10% global tariff under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974, at an estimated cost of ~$1,500 per US household in 2026.
  • EU/UK Iran sanctions snapback. The EU and UK activated sanctions snapbacks on Iran’s energy and financial sectors, aligning with US measures and further constraining Iranian oil export revenue.
