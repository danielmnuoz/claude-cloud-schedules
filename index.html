Middle East — Iran War (Developing)

  • On February 28, 2026, the US and Israel launched "Operation Epic Fury," striking sites across Iran and killing Supreme Leader Khamenei. Iran retaliated with missile and drone strikes against Israel, US bases, and allied nations.
  • Iran closed the Strait of Hormuz, stranding ~20 mb/d of Gulf oil exports — the IEA calls it the largest supply disruption in global oil market history. The conflict spread into Lebanon (1,400+ killed by early April); Houthi involvement raises further risk of Bab al-Mandab closure.
  • A US waiver allowing sale of Russian oil loaded before March 12 expires April 11. Saudi Arabia warns Brent could reach $180/bbl if disruptions persist through late April.

Ukraine-Russia

  • Russia claimed full control of Ukraine's Luhansk region (April 1) and has captured ~1,927 sq miles of territory over the past 12 months. Russian military casualties since Feb. 2022 estimated at ~1.3 million (killed + wounded).
  • Ukraine conducted long-range drone strikes on Russian oil infrastructure in the Baltic region, halting ~40% of Russia's oil export capacity; allies have asked Kyiv to pause refinery strikes given global fuel price pressures from the Iran war.
  • US envoys Witkoff and Kushner expected in Kyiv this month for peace talks; Ukraine insists on a ceasefire at current front lines.

Trade Policy

  • On April 2 ("Liberation Day" anniversary), Trump issued new tariffs including up to 100% tariffs on branded pharmaceuticals and adjustments to steel, aluminum, and copper under Section 232.
  • One year into the tariff regime: factory jobs are down, inflation is up, but the trade deficit has declined for 10 consecutive months as 20+ trading partners opened their markets.
