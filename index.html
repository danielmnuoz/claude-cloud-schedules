US–Iran War — Ceasefire on the Brink (Developing)

  • Ceasefire expires Wednesday evening ET — Trump says it is "highly unlikely" he will extend it; Iran's Foreign Ministry says continued US "provocative actions" are a major obstacle to further diplomacy. Both sides are accusing each other of violations.
  • US Navy seized Iranian cargo ship TOUSKA in the Gulf of Oman after it attempted to breach the US naval blockade — the most serious ceasefire incident to date, escalating risk of resumed full hostilities.
  • Second peace-talk round planned in Islamabad (Pakistan), following the failed April 12 meeting between VP Vance and Iranian FM Araghchi. Iran has not confirmed attendance. Trajectory: Conflict resumption is the base-case risk if no deal materialises by Wednesday; oil would spike to $100+ and equities would sell off sharply.

Trade Policy & Tariffs

  • Trump threatened 50% tariffs on China after reports emerged that Beijing may be preparing a weapons shipment to Iran, adding a new escalation front to the ongoing trade war. No formal order yet, but markets are watching closely.
  • Section 301 investigations into structural excess capacity across 16+ countries (China, EU, Japan, Vietnam, Mexico, and others) head to USTR public hearings on April 28. Comments closed April 15.
  • Trump–Xi summit possible in late April/early May — could serve as a diplomatic off-ramp on tariff threats, though China's potential Iran arms deal significantly complicates prospects.

Tech & Business

  • Apple names John Ternus as next CEO, effective September 1, 2026. Tim Cook, 65, transitions to Executive Chairman — Apple's first CEO change since Cook succeeded Steve Jobs in 2011. Ternus, 50, is SVP of Hardware Engineering and led the Apple Silicon chip transition. AAPL futures were mixed on the news; Cook remains in the role through the summer transition.
  • Tech industry shed ~96,000 jobs in 2026 YTD across 249+ companies. Nearly 48% of cuts are explicitly attributed to AI-driven automation displacing headcount. Amazon (~16,000), Oracle (10,000–30,000 total), Snap (~1,000), and Disney (~1,000) are among the largest cutters.
  • AI restructuring is now the dominant rationale for tech layoffs, with companies redirecting budgets from human headcount to AI infrastructure build-out. Some analysts dispute the framing, noting post-pandemic over-hiring as a concurrent cause.
  • Tesla Q1 2026 earnings hit Wednesday after close — analysts expect $22.3B revenue (+15.5% YoY) and EPS of $0.36 (+33.3% YoY). This is arguably the week's most watched print, with investors focused on EV demand trends and any update on AI/robotics revenue timelines.
