Iran-US Conflict (Day 45) — Top Story

  • Weekend peace talks in Islamabad collapsed on April 12 after Iran rejected US "red lines" including full cessation of uranium enrichment, dismantlement of enrichment facilities, and US retrieval of Iran's highly enriched uranium. VP JD Vance confirmed no deal was reached.
  • President Trump announced the US military will blockade the Strait of Hormuz, effective 10am ET today (April 13), blocking all maritime traffic entering or exiting Iranian ports. The Strait carries roughly 20% of the world's oil supply; shipping activity is currently reported at less than 10% of normal levels.
  • Trajectory / Implications: Analysts warn this blockade is the most severe disruption to global energy supply chains since 2022. It risks pushing Brent crude above $110–120/bbl, materially worsening global inflation, and forcing central banks — including the Fed — toward rate hikes rather than cuts. UN Security Council is expected to convene an emergency session this week.

Trade, Sanctions & Diplomacy

  • The Hormuz blockade effectively places Iran under near-total maritime isolation; secondary effects on global LNG, petrochemical, and container shipping routes are still being assessed.
  • A Trump visit to China remains planned for April; a US-China trade-truce formalization could act as a partial offset to the Iran-driven risk-off, though timing is now uncertain given the Middle East escalation.
  • The USTR's Section 301 investigations covering 50+ countries for forced-labor enforcement failures have a public comment deadline this week (Apr 15).

Tech & Business

Layoffs & AI-Driven Workforce Reduction

  • The tech industry cut ~80,000 jobs in Q1 2026 across 146 events; year-to-date, ~99,000 workers have been affected, with over 76% of roles US-based. ~48% of cuts are explicitly attributed to AI and workflow automation.
  • Oracle laid off an estimated 20,000–30,000 employees via a terse 6am email, one of the largest single-company reductions in recent memory, redirecting capital toward AI data center buildout.
  • Block (Jack Dorsey) eliminated 4,000 jobs — roughly 40% of its global workforce — citing growing AI capability; this is the single largest AI-attributed layoff event in tech history.
  • OpenAI CEO Sam Altman acknowledged "AI washing" (blaming AI for cuts that would have happened anyway) while affirming real displacement is also occurring. IBM is reportedly tripling entry-level hiring, arguing AI still needs human oversight.

AI Model Launches & Product News

  • Microsoft AI released three foundational models — MAI-Transcribe-1, MAI-Voice-1, and MAI-Image-2 — positioned as lower-cost alternatives to Google and OpenAI equivalents.
