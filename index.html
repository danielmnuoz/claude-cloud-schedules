US–Iran War — Day 48 (Trajectory: De-escalating)

  • Background: The US and Israel launched coordinated airstrikes on Iran on February 28, 2026, killing Supreme Leader Khamenei; the conflict has caused thousands of deaths, millions of displaced persons, and severe Strait of Hormuz disruption.
  • Ceasefire (Apr 8): A 2-week US–Iran ceasefire brokered by Pakistan took effect April 8. Both sides have committed violations since its declaration; the ceasefire expires April 21.
  • Lebanon front (Apr 16): A separate Israel–Lebanon ceasefire went into effect overnight; celebratory fire lit up Beirut’s skyline. UN Secretary-General urged respect for international law and continuity of the agreement.
  • Peace talks (Apr 17): Trump said a permanent Iran deal is “very close” and that a second round of talks in Islamabad could happen “this weekend.” Key reported US demands include Iran abandoning nuclear ambitions and reopening the Strait of Hormuz; Iranian officials have not publicly confirmed the terms.
