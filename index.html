Iran War & Middle East (Day 42–43)

  • A two-week US-Iran ceasefire was announced April 8, brokered by Pakistan. Trump said Iran would immediately open the Strait of Hormuz and work toward a peace deal, sparking the week's relief rally. Over 3,400 have been killed in the conflict, including 1,500+ in Lebanon and 13 US service members.
  • Talks in Islamabad (Apr 11–12): VP Vance, Steve Witkoff, and Jared Kushner are leading the US side; Iran's delegation (Foreign Minister Araghchi, Parliament Speaker Ghalibaf) has rejected a temporary ceasefire and presented a 10-point counter-proposal demanding sanctions relief, reconstruction funding, a regional conflict resolution framework, and a protocol to re-open Hormuz.
  • Lebanon remains a flashpoint: Netanyahu has explicitly excluded Lebanon from the ceasefire, directly contradicting Pakistani PM Sharif's claim that it covered Israeli operations there. Iran's Ghalibaf blamed the US for "failing to gain trust" in the talks.
  • Trajectory: Ceasefire is highly contested. A breakdown risks oil re-spiking to $105–110/bbl and renewed market selloff. A comprehensive deal would be a major bullish catalyst. Watch Islamabad talks closely over the coming days.
