International Markets & Geopolitics

2026 Iran War & Strait of Hormuz Crisis

  • Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz closed on March 4, triggering the largest oil supply disruption in the history of global energy markets — roughly 4.5–5 million barrels/day lost (~5% of global supply), a figure analysts warn could double by mid-April.
  • Brent crude settled at $118/bbl Tuesday after a strike on a Kuwaiti tanker; WTI settled near $101. Dubai crude hit a record $166/bbl on March 19. Oil executives warn the Strait must reopen by mid-April or disruptions worsen severely.
  • Iran's Pezeshkian signaled conditional willingness to negotiate, sparking Tuesday's equity rally, but no ceasefire materialized and the IRGC has reaffirmed the strait remains closed. The trajectory remains highly uncertain — Goldman and others have raised $200/bbl as a tail risk.
  • The US has released 400 million barrels from strategic reserves (record), temporarily lifted sanctions on some Russian/Iranian oil, and is participating in a French-led naval escort mission (Operation Aspides). Qatar's LNG plant sustained missile damage — repairs estimated to take up to 5 years.
